Reduce Demand

When demand is reduced, wildlife trade will decrease.

Activities include, 
 
1) Behaviour change campaigns that reward responsible consumerism; 
2) Develop alternative livelihood programs for communities dependent on wildlife consumption or sales;
3) Strengthening laws and policies.

Protect and Expand Habitat

Keeping wild animals and their habitat secure from poaching, logging, mining, and encroachment.

Activities include, 

1) Frontline ranger and community patrol support: training, equipment, life insurance;
2) Poverty reduction: youth education and alternative livelihood support;
3) Empower rural communities to defend their land and human rights as protectors of nature;
4) Restoring nature: growing protected areas, buffer zones corridors and rewilding;
5) Sustainable agriculture: scaling environmentally friendly farming practices.

Counter Trafficking

Freezing the financial trigger on poaching.

Activities include, 

1) Empowering law enforcers to identify and dismantle criminal syndicates and seize assets;
2) Funding wildlife enforcement operations;
3) Establishing and linking data-bases on wildlife crimes;
4) Helping the private sector adopt compliance measures for nature protection;
5) Enable and reward citizen support for, and monitoring of, nature protection.
